# ISH Gifting Rules & Guidelines

## Core offering
India Sweet House offers premium pure-ghee mithai, dry fruit hampers, and curated gift boxes for corporate gifting.
Pricing:
- 50–200 units: ₹700–₹1,200 per person
- 201–1,000 units: ₹500–₹900 per person
- 1,001+ units: ₹400–₹750 per person (bulk pricing)

## Product catalogue (key SKUs)
- Signature Mithai Box (500g): Kaju Barfi, Motichoor Ladoo, Mysore Pak
- Premium Dry Fruit Hamper (500g): Almonds, Cashews, Pistachios, Walnuts
- Festival Combo Box: Mithai + Dry Fruits + Saffron
- Luxe Corporate Box: Premium packaging, company logo on lid, silk ribbon

## Diwali campaign guidelines
Season window: Mid-October to November 15 (peak order period: Sep 15 – Oct 15)
- Lead time recommended: 4–6 weeks before Diwali date
- Minimum order: 50 boxes
- Customisation: Company name / logo on box lid, greeting card inside
- Delivery: Pan-India for orders > 200 units; Bangalore same-day for < 200 units
- Shelf life: Mithai 15 days; Dry fruit combos 3 months
- Payment: 50% advance on order confirmation; 50% before dispatch

## Target buyer personas
- HR Director / HR Manager: Primary buyer for employee gifting
- Admin Head / Office Manager: Ops-level execution buyer
- Procurement Manager: Budget approval + vendor empanelment
- CEO/MD at SMBs (< 500 employees): Decision + budget holder directly

## Industries with highest gifting index
1. IT / ITES / Software (high employee count, culture of gifting)
2. Manufacturing & Industrial (strong tradition of Diwali bonuses + gifts)
3. Real Estate & Construction (client + employee gifting)
4. BFSI (Banking, Financial Services, Insurance)
5. Pharma & Healthcare (employee welfare focus)
6. Retail chains (staff gifting)

## Outreach tone guidelines
- Friendly but professional. Not salesy, no fluff, no people-pleasing.
- Subject line: specific, under 50 characters, include company name OR contact first name
- Body: max 3 sentences in pitch (excluding greeting and sign-off), under 120 words total
- Para 1: Hook with one sourced detail (industry, company intel, or role)
- Para 2: Plain product/value line tied to brand vertical (sweets or appliances per brand config)
- Para 3: One low-friction question CTA (e.g. "Open to a quick note on options?")
- Sign-off: First name, role, company (e.g. "Priya\nPartnerships, India Sweet House")
- Avoid: buzzwords, hype, flattery, filler openers ("Hope this finds you well")
- Avoid: multiple CTAs, hard meeting asks in email 1, competitor mentions

## Compliance
- Include unsubscribe / opt-out line at footer of every email
- Do not mention competitor sweets brands
- Do not claim health benefits or certifications not verified
- GDPR-equivalent: honor opt-out requests within 24 hours
- Audit trail: log every send event

## Objection handling (quick reference)
- "Too expensive": Offer sample pack first; emphasise per-person cost at scale
- "Already have a vendor": Ask about customisation options they currently get
- "Too early": Remind that premium orders book 4–6 weeks out
- "Send brochure": Offer 2-min video instead + follow up in 3 days
